Hobby

  • You do not need to keep records of your hobby for the ATO.
  • It’s good practice to keep records in case your circumstances change e.g. keeping records of assets you own for your hobby as you may be able to claim for these after you start your business.
  • You'll need to keep records to meet your consumer law requirements when buying and selling goods or services e.g. to give receipts or refunds etc.

You may need to keep records to report your income if you:

  • receive government payments, such as from Centrelink or the Department of Veterans’ Affairs (DVA)
  • make payments where income is considered, such as for child support.

Business

You need to keep records:

  • for the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) to meet your obligations. It also makes it easier to manage your cash flow and business finances. You should keep records of assets you own for your hobby as you may be able to claim for these after you start your business
  • for consumer law requirements e.g. to give receipts or refunds
  • to meet your business structure obligations e.g. If you’re running a company, you may have additional record keeping requirements.

You may also need to keep records to report your income if you:

  • receive government payments, such as from Centrelink or the Department of Veterans’ Affairs (DVA)
  • make payments where income is considered, such as for child support.

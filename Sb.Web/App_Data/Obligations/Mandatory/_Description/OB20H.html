You do not need to declare the amounts you make from your hobby to the Australian Taxation Office (ATO). You cannot claim deductions for expenses you incur for your hobby.

If you rely on this tool for tax purposes, the ATO’s commitment statement applies to you. Make sure you keep a copy of the outcome you get under the tool.

This tool only applies to money you’re getting from making creative works. What you need to declare for tax purposes may change if you are getting money from doing other things with your creative work, such as teaching or performing.

Find out about:

  • income you need to declare
  • personal services income (PSI)
  • declaring money you earn from videos you upload on YouTube

However, you will need to report the amounts you earn from your hobby if you:

  • receive certain payments, such as from Centrelink or the Department of Veterans’ Affairs (DVA)
  • make payments where income is considered, such as for child support.

Read more about income reporting on the Department of Human Services website.

Read more about notifying DVA on the Department of Veterans’ Affairs website.
